— Which One Is Right for You?

Both apps help Indian landlords manage rentals. But they're built for very different needs. This side-by-side comparison covers features, pricing, and who each app actually suits best.

RentyBase wins for landlords who want HRA receipts, deposit management, move-in photo proof, and a free tenant-facing experience. NoBroker Rent Manager is better if you're already a NoBroker broker customer and just want basic rent collection. RentyBase is free for both parties; NoBroker charges landlords monthly.

The Detail: Where RentyBase Wins

HRA Receipt Compliance

This is the biggest difference. Every March, millions of Indian salaried employees scramble for HRA receipts to submit to their employer's HR department. The Income Tax Act requires receipts to include the landlord's PAN if annual rent exceeds ₹1 lakh — which it does for most city rentals.

RentyBase receipts include: landlord PAN, tenant name, full property address with PIN code, rent amount in numerals and words (Indian lakh format), payment method and UTR, and a landlord signature placeholder. All required fields, automatically filled.

NoBroker's rent receipts often lack the landlord's PAN field and don't display rent in words — meaning many tenants find their HR department rejects the receipt during declaration season.

Security Deposit Disputes

Deposit disputes are the #1 source of landlord-tenant conflict in India. NoBroker doesn't offer any deposit management features — it's purely rent collection. RentyBase includes:

  • A transparent deposit ledger visible to both landlord and tenant
  • Move-in photo documentation per room (kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, etc.)
  • Deductions logged with reason and photo evidence
  • Timestamped records that can't be altered retroactively

When a landlord claims the tenant "damaged" the bathroom tiles, move-in photos prove the tiles were already cracked before the tenant moved in. This single feature prevents thousands of rupees in wrongful deductions.

Repair Request Tracking

In the NoBroker Rent Manager, there's no feature for repair requests. Tenants text their landlord on WhatsApp, where messages get buried. No follow-up mechanism, no accountability.

RentyBase has a dedicated repair request module where tenants raise requests with photos, landlords see them on their dashboard, and both parties can see the status timeline. Requests don't "disappear" — they stay open until resolved.

Pricing

RentyBase is completely free for both landlords and tenants. NoBroker Rent Manager charges landlords ₹999–₹2,999 per year depending on the plan tier. For a landlord managing 2–3 properties, this adds up — and the features you're paying for (better HRA receipts, deposit management) are available free on RentyBase.

When NoBroker Makes More Sense

NoBroker's rent management is a natural add-on if you're already using NoBroker to find tenants (their core business). If you found your tenant through NoBroker listings, it's seamless to continue managing the rent collection through the same platform. For purely transactional rent collection — just getting money in — NoBroker's UPI-based system works fine.

NoBroker also has a larger brand presence, so if your tenant is more comfortable with a well-known platform, that can reduce friction during onboarding.

Verdict

If your priority is rent collection only, both apps work. But if you care about HRA compliance, deposit protection, move-in documentation, and repair tracking — RentyBase is the better choice, and it's free. The total feature set at zero cost makes it the clear recommendation for Indian landlords managing one or more properties.
